What is UUID?

Universally unique identifier (UUID)

It is a 128-bit label used for information in computer systems.

When generated according to the standard methods, UUIDs are, for practical purposes, unique. Their uniqueness does not depend on a central registration authority or coordination between the parties generating them, unlike most other numbering schemes. While the probability that a UUID will be duplicated is not zero, it is close enough to zero to be negligible. The probability of a UUID being duplicated is at most 2-128 (1 in 1838).
